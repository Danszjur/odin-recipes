Gulyás Soup

Description

Gulyás soup (often called “goulash”) is a traditional Hungarian dish, famous for its rich flavor and hearty ingredients. Originally prepared by herdsmen, this soup combines tender beef, root vegetables, potatoes, and the distinctive taste of Hungarian paprika to create a warming and satisfying meal. It is typically enjoyed as a main course and is perfect for family gatherings or cold days. The balance of spices and slow cooking bring out deep, comforting flavors that represent the essence of Hungarian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 500 g (1 lb) beef (shank, shoulder, or stew meat), cut into cubes
  • 2 tbsp lard or vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp Hungarian sweet paprika
  • 1 tsp caraway seeds
  • 2 medium carrots, sliced
  • 2 medium parsnips, sliced
  • 2 medium potatoes, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes or 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1.5 liters (6 cups) beef broth or water
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat the lard or oil in a large pot. Add onions and sauté until golden.
  2. Stir in the garlic and paprika, mixing quickly to avoid burning.
  3. Add the beef cubes, season with salt and pepper, and brown on all sides.
  4. Pour in a small amount of broth or water and simmer until the meat begins to soften.
  5. Add the caraway seeds, carrots, parsnips, bell pepper, and tomatoes. Stir well.
  6. Pour in the remaining broth or water. Cover and simmer gently for about 1–1.5 hours, until the beef is tender.
  7. Add the diced potatoes and cook for another 20 minutes, until soft.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  9. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley, alongside crusty bread.
